astḗr
Ástra

Literally, “star”. Curiously, the proper plural, astéres, is scarce used; the collective Ástra ​—​strictly the plural of ástron​—​serves in its place, as though the night-twinkling Stars formed a single host rather than a multitude of stars. No relation to ‹Astártē›, astrágalos. Cognate of ‹star›; related to the cognates of ‹astronomy›, ‹asterisk›, ‹stellar›, ‹Stella›.
